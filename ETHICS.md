# Developer ethics and responsible research

This document is a **self-attested public commitment**, not a third-party certification.

I use it as a baseline for personal development, open-source work, AI-assisted development, and security research.

## Core principles

### Authorized scope

- Test only systems, repositories, programs, and environments where I have permission.
- Follow the published scope, rules, rate limits, and disclosure requirements of each program.
- Stop testing when authorization or impact is unclear.

### Privacy and data minimization

- Avoid collecting personal data, credentials, secrets, or production content unless strictly necessary to demonstrate an authorized issue.
- Store the minimum evidence needed and remove sensitive data as soon as it is no longer required.
- Never publish secrets or unnecessary personal information in reports, issues, commits, logs, or demonstrations.

### Responsible security research

- Report vulnerabilities through the designated private channel whenever one is available.
- Give maintainers a reasonable opportunity to investigate and fix an issue before public disclosure.
- Do not provide exploit material that unnecessarily increases the risk of abuse.
- Distinguish confirmed impact from assumptions and clearly state environmental limitations.

### Reproducibility and honesty

- Preserve relevant test steps, logs, versions, failure conditions, and expected results where practical.
- Do not present unexecuted tests, inferred results, or experimental software as verified facts.
- Clearly label experimental, unaudited, incomplete, or security-sensitive work.

### Open-source conduct

- Respect project contribution guidelines, codes of conduct, licenses, maintainers, and reviewers.
- Keep pull requests focused and provide enough evidence for maintainers to review the change independently.
- Avoid unsolicited experiments that could waste maintainer time, disrupt infrastructure, or create risk for users.

### AI-assisted development

- Review generated code and claims before submitting or publishing them.
- Do not use AI tools to bypass authorization, conceal harmful behavior, or manufacture contribution history.
- Treat generated output as untrusted until it has been tested and inspected.
